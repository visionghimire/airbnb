An electric guitar is a guitar that requires external amplification in order to be heard at typical performance volumes. It uses one or more pickups to convert the vibration of its strings into electrical signals, which ultimately are reproduced as sound by loudspeakers.

Acoustic

An acoustic guitar is a musical instrument in the guitar family. Its strings vibrate a sound board on a resonant body to project a sound wave through the air. Acoustic music is music that solely or primarily uses instruments that produce sound through acoustic means.

Bass Guitar

The bass guitar, electric bass, or simply bass, is the lowest-pitched member of the guitar family. It is a plucked string instrument similar in appearance to an electric or an acoustic guitar, but with a longer neck and scale length, and typically four to six strings or courses.
